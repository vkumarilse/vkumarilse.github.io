CC3-Policy Debate: Income Inequality in India

India's income inequality has deepened dramatically since 1951. The line chart reveals a stark divergence: the top 1%'s share doubled from 11.5% to 22.6%, while the top 10% captured 57.7% of national income by 2024. Despite rising absolute incomes, the bottom 50% saw their relative share decline, highlighting concerns about inclusive growth and widening economic divides.
